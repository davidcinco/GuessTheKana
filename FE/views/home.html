What is Hiragana and Katakana?

Why do Japanese characters look so different from each other? In fact, Japanese has three different writing systems: hiragana, katakana, and kanji.
These are Japanese syllabary, part of the Japanese writing system, along with kanji. Hiragana and katakana are both kana systems. With few exceptions, each mora in the Japanese language is represented by one character (or one digraph) in each system.
